# Organisational Archetypes

Character types that populate the satire. Mix, layer, or subvert them as needed.

---

## The Visionary

**Role**: Founder, C-suite, or anyone who attended one TED Talk and never recovered.

**Traits**:
- Speaks in metaphors about oceans, fires, and "10x thinking"
- Has never met a deadline they couldn't "reframe as a learning"
- Owns at least one book by Elon Musk, Jeff Bezos, or a Stoic philosopher
- Loves the word "moonshot" without any awareness of what it takes to reach the moon

**Speech patterns**:
> "We're not building software. We're building *the future*."
> "I don't want to manage processes. I want to *shape destiny*."
> "Failure is just success that hasn't pivoted yet."

**Satirical angle**: The gap between their language and their spreadsheets. They speak in epochs; they operate in quarters.

---

## The Middle Manager

**Role**: Team lead, department head, "people manager" — the load-bearing wall of corporate dysfunction.

**Traits**:
- Exists primarily to relay information between people who could talk directly
- Deeply invested in the appearance of busyness
- Sends emails at 11pm to signal dedication; replies to none before 10am
- Learned "psychological safety" in a workshop, now deploys it as a shield

**Speech patterns**:
> "Let's take this offline."
> "I want to make sure everyone has space to share."
> "Great question — I'll follow up on that." *(never follows up)*

**Satirical angle**: Sincere effort, unclear purpose. They are not villains. That's what makes it funny.

---

## The LinkedIn Luminary

**Role**: Senior IC, ex-founder, or anyone who discovered personal branding.

**Traits**:
- Announces personal losses as professional growth
- Posts about "lessons from failure" the same week the company layoffs 15% of staff
- Describes ordinary tasks as journeys, ordinary outcomes as transformations
- Uses "we" when things go well; uses "I learned" when things don't

**Speech patterns**:
> "Three years ago, I was burned out, broke, and confused. Today, I run a $4M ARR business. Here's what I learned: 🧵"
> "Hot take: burnout is just misaligned values."
> "No one talks about this, but..."

**Satirical angle**: The performance of vulnerability as a marketing strategy. Authenticity, industrialised.

---

## The Process Evangelist

**Role**: Operations, project management, anyone who has attended a Scrum certification.

**Traits**:
- Believes every problem is a workflow problem
- Introduced Notion, then Jira, then Linear, then Notion again
- Sends the agenda 10 minutes before the meeting they forgot to cancel
- "I just want to make sure we're aligned on the alignment process"

**Speech patterns**:
> "Before we start — can we level-set on the goal of this conversation?"
> "I've mapped this out in a Miro board."
> "We should probably do a retro on the retro."

**Satirical angle**: The belief that chaos can be organised away, and the chaos this belief produces.

---

## The Eternal Optimist

**Role**: HR, culture team, Chief People Officer, or anyone with "happiness" in their title.

**Traits**:
- Responds to bad news with activities
- Believes team morale can be fixed with pizza, Slack emojis, or a photo booth
- Describes layoffs as "right-sizing" and restructures as "an exciting opportunity"
- Genuinely hurt when the team doesn't want to play trivia at 5pm on a Friday

**Speech patterns**:
> "I'm hearing a lot of energy in this room!"
> "What if we framed this as a *challenge*?"
> "The survey results are in and people are... cautiously optimistic!"

**Satirical angle**: Optimism as institutional defence mechanism. Not malicious. Possibly worse.

---

## The Burned-Out IC

**Role**: Engineer, analyst, writer, designer — anyone who has been at the company long enough to remember "the old way".

**Traits**:
- Has seen three reorgs and stopped updating their LinkedIn
- Answers every new initiative with "we tried this in 2019"
- Technically competent; spiritually absent
- The only person in the all-hands asking the question everyone else is thinking

**Speech patterns**:
> "...wasn't this what Horizon was supposed to do?"
> *(silence during brainstorms)*
> "Sure. I'll add it to the backlog."

**Satirical angle**: Institutional memory as a kind of grief. They're not wrong. That's the joke.

---

## The New Hire

**Role**: Anyone in their first 90 days.

**Traits**:
- Enthusiastic enough to ask why things are done this way
- Gets a different answer from every person they ask
- Books 1:1s with everyone; learns nothing of use
- Will either be absorbed into the culture or leave before their probation ends

**Speech patterns**:
> "I was reading the company handbook and I had a question—"
> "At my last place, we used to—"
> "Oh. I see. That makes sense." *(it does not make sense)*

**Satirical angle**: Fresh eyes see the absurdity clearly. The system's job is to fix that.

---

## The Consultant

**Role**: External advisor, agency lead, "transformation partner".

**Traits**:
- Charges enterprise rates to say what the ICs said in 2019
- Presents findings in decks with very large fonts and very few numbers
- Always available for another workshop
- Refers to the client company as "you folks" with barely concealed pity

**Speech patterns**:
> "What we're seeing is a maturity gap."
> "In our experience working with organisations at your stage..."
> "The good news is, this is very fixable."

**Satirical angle**: The outsider who sees clearly and acts on nothing. Paid to observe, not to stay.
