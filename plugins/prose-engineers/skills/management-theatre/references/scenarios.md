# Satirical Scenarios

Common organisational events and the specific absurdities they produce. Each entry covers the official version, the actual version, and angles for satire.

---

## The All-Hands Meeting

**Official purpose**: Align the company, celebrate wins, share the roadmap.

**What actually happens**:
- 40 minutes of slides the CEO is reading for the first time
- A Q&A section that ends before anyone asks the real question
- A metric that looks like growth if you squint and ignore the denominator
- "Any other questions?" asked in a tone that means "please no"

**Satirical angles**:
- The gap between the slide headline and the footnote
- The careful choreography of who sits near the front
- The chat box running parallel commentary the speakers cannot see
- The employee who asks a real question and watches the room go quiet

## OKR Season

**Official purpose**: Align individual goals with company strategy for the quarter.

**What actually happens**:
- A week of meetings to agree on what we already agreed on last quarter
- Key results written to be achievable before they're written
- A 70% attainment framed as "ambitious by design"
- The one team whose OKRs don't connect to anything discovering this in November

**Satirical angles**:
- The OKR that measures process (ran 12 meetings) instead of outcome (changed anything)
- The strategic priority that has been Priority 1 for six consecutive quarters
- The exercise of reverse-engineering OKRs from things already done
- "Aspirational" as a synonym for "we are not doing this"

## The Reorg

**Official purpose**: Better alignment, clearer ownership, organisational efficiency.

**What actually happens**:
- The same people reporting to different people doing the same things
- Three weeks of uncertainty during which no one does anything
- An announcement email with phrases like "exciting new chapter" and "stronger together"
- A new org chart that will be out of date before it's printed

**Satirical angles**:
- The reorg that reorganises the team that just reorganised
- Reporting lines as political theatre
- The job title that changes; the job that doesn't
- "You'll be seeing more of these changes in the coming months" (everyone updates their LinkedIn)

## The Team-Building Offsite

**Official purpose**: Connection, creativity, strategic alignment outside the day-to-day.

**What actually happens**:
- A trust exercise between people who will never fully trust each other
- A brainstorm session whose outputs are photographed and never seen again
- An evening social where attendance is optional and absence is noted
- A shared Google Doc of "action items" that no one has edit permissions for

**Satirical angles**:
- The icebreaker that takes longer than the strategy session
- The "fun" activity that requires people to be good at something they were not warned about
- The offsite at a location that requires a 6am flight
- Genuine connection forming over shared frustration at the offsite itself

## The Retrospective

**Official purpose**: Reflect on what went well, what didn't, and how to improve.

**What actually happens**:
- "What went well" takes 25 minutes; "what could be better" takes 4
- The same issues from last retro appear under different sticky note colors
- Actions are assigned; no one writes down who owns them
- "We should probably do this more regularly" said at the end of every retro

**Satirical angles**:
- The retro action that becomes next retro's "what could be better"
- The facilitator trying to keep it positive while the team is clearly not fine
- The ritual of honesty performed in a space designed to limit it
- The retro held after a project that the team knows will be repeated immediately

## The Performance Review

**Official purpose**: Honest feedback, career development, calibration.

**What actually happens**:
- Feedback so carefully worded it communicates nothing
- A rating system where "meets expectations" means "not getting a raise"
- A conversation about growth that was scheduled the week after the promotion decision was made
- Self-review forms that reward those who are good at describing themselves

**Satirical angles**:
- The manager writing "strong communicator" for someone who has never communicated anything
- The calibration meeting where one person's exceptional becomes another's meets expectations
- The 360 feedback from a colleague who hasn't worked with you this year
- "We really value you here" said in a year with no budget for compensation adjustments

## The Product Launch

**Official purpose**: Ship something, tell the world, measure adoption.

**What actually happens**:
- A launch date chosen for the board deck, not the product
- A press release that anthropomorphises software ("our platform *thinks*...")
- Metrics redefined post-launch to show the graph trending up
- Customers who were promised the feature discovering it is a checkbox in a settings menu

**Satirical angles**:
- The demo that works exactly once, in controlled conditions, for the CEO
- The announcement blog post that uses the word "delighted" three times
- The difference between the launch announcement and the week-two usage numbers
- The team celebration before anyone has looked at actual retention

## The Hiring Freeze

**Official purpose**: Financial discipline, cost management, runway extension.

**What actually happens**:
- Four open roles quietly disappear from the website
- The team carries the workload; the word "temporary" is repeated
- New contractors appear; they are not technically "headcount"
- A hiring plan for Q3 that exists primarily as a retention mechanism

**Satirical angles**:
- The freeze announcement paired with a leadership hire three weeks later
- "We're not backfilling the role" meaning the role still exists, just unpaid
- The contractor who has been "temporary" for two years
- Gallows humour in the team Slack about what the next cost-saving measure will be

## The Remote-First Meeting

**Official purpose**: Include distributed team members equitably in decision-making.

**What actually happens**:
- Four people in a conference room, one camera pointed at a whiteboard, three on Zoom
- The in-room conversation running ten minutes ahead of the shared screen
- "Can everyone see my screen?" asked four times
- Decisions made informally in the room, presented as conclusions on the call

**Satirical angles**:
- The muted participant who is definitely still eating
- The person on video who has clearly switched to another tab
- The dog/child/delivery person interruption treated with serious inconvenience
- "Let's circle back in the thread" — the thread in which nothing ever circles back

## The Strategy Day

**Official purpose**: Define direction, prioritise bets, align leadership.

**What actually happens**:
- A 9am start delayed because someone is still on a flight
- Three frameworks applied sequentially to the same problem
- A list of priorities so long it is not a list of priorities
- "Strategic" used as an adjective that upgrades any noun ("strategic snacks")

**Satirical angles**:
- The facilitator whose neutrality is doing a lot of heavy lifting
- The strategy that emerges looking identical to last year's strategy with new fonts
- The "parking lot" of issues that were parked in Q1 and have not moved
- The moment the most important conversation happens in the bathroom between sessions

## The AI Oracle

**Official purpose**: Speed up development, reduce boilerplate, unblock engineers.

**What actually happens**:
- A decision gets made; the reasoning gets replaced by "the agent said so"
- Someone questions the output; someone else re-asks the same agent and gets the same answer, cited as confirmation
- The original human judgment quietly exits the room
- The PR gets merged; the comment says `// per recommendation`

**Satirical angles**:
- Outsourced reasoning as a social contract — if the tool is wrong, no one is wrong
- The circular validation loop (ask → get answer → re-ask to confirm → cite confirmation)
- Accountability that has no address
- The team that ships confidently and can't explain a single decision in the codebase
